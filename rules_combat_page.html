Intro:

Combat, Conversations, and Conundrums are the three pillars of play that this game is designed around. All are treated the same way via the pool mechanic. You effectively have three healthbars that interact with these three concepts.

Conversations and conundrums share similar mechanics with the combat system to encourage diverse playstyles. No longer is it absolutely necessary that everyone is outfitted to kill the biggest baddest beast around, doing so will leave gaping holes in the party's skillset.

Combat:

This system is still up in the air. There are like a million ways to do it.

Option 1: Every character simply has a "difficulty to hit" or DtH, which functions exactly as armor class does in d&d. You first make a roll using the combat skill. If you roll higher than the opponents DtHx2, you hit. Then you roll for damage, which usually relies purely on the type of weapon and your strength attribute.

Option 2: Only PCs roll. When a PC is targetted by an incoming attack, they must make a choice. Do they try to dodge, block, or parry? Dodging means you must make an acrobatics skill check, the difficulty is a set number determined by the enemy's attributes and weapon. Dodging in inhibited by armor and also rmeoves 1AP that you would normally regenerate next turn. Blocking means you try to absorb all, or at least some, of the damage. You gain armor (functions as DR) determined as 1d6 + modifiers from armor and shields. Parrying is high risk high reward. You make a check, if you succeed then you avoid all damage and your next strike is an automatic hit. If you fail you are hit for full damage.

Option 3: Secret third option idk.


Conversation:

There are two major modals of play dring conversations. 1 - roleplay conversations, this subverts the need for skill checks by using the PC's arguments and words to move the plot along. 2 - Using social abilities to influence NPCs. There are spell-like abilities called social abilities that can influence a conversation in set ways. These abilities generally accomplish things that might otherwise be nearly impossible, like convincing a hostile guard that arresting the PCs just isn't worth the trouble. Rather than a long, belabored roleplay to convince the guard of this, you can choose to perform a "manipulation" ability for several Charisma pool points OR you can attempt a diplomacy check which has a chance of failure.


Conundrums:

Conundrum are all the puzzles and difficult decisions you encounter during play. You can expend mental on thinking really really hard about things, potentially at the detriment of your intelligence pool. Thinking too much can burn out your brain after all, you will lose consciousness if you expend your mind too much.

Some mental abilities, such as "insight" will reveal truths about the world if the user can pass certain checks.
